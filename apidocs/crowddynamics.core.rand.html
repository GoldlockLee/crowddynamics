crowddynamics.core.rand.functions.poisson_clock[source]¶

Generated points in time using Poisson process.

Time between updates are independent random variables defined

\[\tau_i \sim \operatorname{Exp}(\lambda)\]

Where the rate parameter is

\[\operatorname{E}(\tau) = \frac{1}{\lambda} = interval\]

Times when agent updates its strategy

\[\begin{split}\begin{split}\begin{cases} T_n = \tau_1 + \ldots + \tau_n, & n \geq 1 \\ T_0 = 0 \end{cases}\end{split}\end{split}\]

Sequence of update times

\[\{T_1, T_2, T_3, \ldots, T_n\}, \quad T_n < \Delta t\]

Number of arrivals by the \(s\)

\[\begin{split}N(s) &= \max\{n : T_n \leq s\} \\ N(s) &\sim \operatorname{Poi}(\lambda)\end{split}\]
Parameters:
  • interval (float) – Expected frequency of update.
  • dt (float) – Discrete timestep \(\Delta t\) aka time window.
Yields:

float – Moments in the time window when the strategies should be updated.

crowddynamics.core.rand.sampling.polygon_sample(vertices)[source]¶

Uniform sampling of points inside a convex polygon. Non convex polygons will be treated as the input would be their convex hull.

Steps of the algorithm

  1. Delaunay triangulation to break the polygon into triangular mesh.
  2. Draw random uniform triangle weighted by its area.
  3. Draw random uniform sample from inside the triangle.
Parameters:vertices (numpy.ndarray) – Array of polygon vertices. Shape of (n, 2).
Yields:numpy.ndarray – Random point inside the polygon.

References

  • http://gis.stackexchange.com/questions/6412/generate-points-that-lie-inside-polygon

crowddynamics.core.rand.sampling.random_sample_triangle[source]¶

Generate uniform random sample from inside of a triangle defined by points \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\). [1]

\[P = (1 - \sqrt{R_1}) \mathbf{a} + (\sqrt{R_1} (1 - R_2)) \mathbf{b} + (R_2 \sqrt{R_1}) \mathbf{c},\]

where uniformly distributed random variables are

\[R_1, R_2 \sim \mathcal{U}(0, 1)\]

Does not work for triangles that have area of zero.

Parameters:
  • a (numpy.ndarray) – Vertex of the triangle
  • b (numpy.ndarray) – Vertex of the triangle
  • c (numpy.ndarray) – Vertex of the triangle
Returns:

Uniformly distributed random point P
Return type:

numpy.ndarray

References

[1]http://math.stackexchange.com/questions/18686/uniform-random-point-in-triangle
